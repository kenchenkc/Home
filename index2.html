Boreal Shield

The climate in the Boreal Shield is suitable for anyone. There are both cold and warm temperatures in the Boreal Shield. Mainly there are long, cold winters and short, warm, summers, making it considered a continental climate. In the winter, the average temperature is -15 degrees celsius, while in the summer, the average temperature is 17 degrees celsius. This makes the temperature range 32 degrees celsius. The annual precipitation ranges from 400 to 1000 millimeters a year. Also, the growing season is around 130 to 190 days.





Ottawa

In Ottawa, the climate is continental as well. This is because Ottawa is not near any large body of water, which can mediate temperature ranges. In fact, the temperature varies between -15 to 26 degrees celsius, which makes the range 41 degrees celsius. On an annual standpoint, the average precipitation (in millimeters) is 895.5. The growing season in Ottawa is around 152 days.






St. John's

St. John’s has almost the exact opposite climate of Ottawa because it is a maritime climate. This means that St. John's experiences cool summers and winters. In the winter, the average temperature is around -1 degrees celsius, while in the summer, the average temperature is 20 degrees celsius. This makes the temperature range 21 degrees celsius. On average, the annual precipitation is 1, 191 millimetres. St. John’s has a very long growing season (over 170 days).
