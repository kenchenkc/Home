Because the Boreal Shield is largely wild and uninhabited by humans, this ecozone is rich with wildlife. Even though Ottawa is a big city, there are still large, natural green spaces where many different animals live. In St. John’s, there are not many varieties of animals. However, this does not mean that these animals are not special.





Animal Species





Boreal Shield

The iconic blue jay lives in the Boreal Shield! (Did you know that Toronto has a baseball team named after these amazing birds?) Furthermore, on the coasts, large whales and seals thrive in the refreshing water. Perhaps, if you are lucky, you may even see one!






Ottawa, Ontario


Many different animals live in Ottawa, such as the

- Canada geese

- Beavers

- White-tailed deer






St. John's, Newfoundland

One animal that is native to St. John’s is the fierce black bear. These black bears love to eat grasses and roots, but hunt for fish and mammals as well.
